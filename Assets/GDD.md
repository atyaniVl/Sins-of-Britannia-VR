# Sins of Britannia VR - Game Design Document (GDD)

Working Title / Polished Version

## 1. Overview

- Title: Sins of Britannia VR (working title)
- Genre: VR Action RPG / Arena Brawler
- Perspective: First-person VR
- Platforms: Meta Quest (Oculus Store, standalone)
- Target Audience: Fans of action anime, VR combat enthusiasts, ages 16+
- Core Fantasy: Wield overwhelming, distinct combat styles inspired by the Seven Deadly Sins, utilizing transforming weapons, magical counters, and devastating ultimates against holy knights and demonic beasts.

## 2. Vision & Pillars

Vision Statement: Deliver a high-impact VR combat experience that captures the overwhelming spectacle and unique mechanics of Nanatsu no Taizai, translating anime physics into satisfying, comfortable VR physicality.

Design Pillars:

- The Overpowering Fantasy: Players shouldn't just feel strong; they should feel like legendary outlaws. Mechanics like perfect counters and massive AoE ground-slams are central.
- Distinct Archetypes (The "Sins"): Each weapon/loadout completely changes the VR interaction model (e.g., fast parrying vs. telekinetic weapon control vs. heavy physical swings).
- VR-First Anime Spectacle: Massive particle effects, time-slows (bullet time), and screen-shattering impacts that remain comfortable in VR.
- The Hub & Fellowship: A cozy, interactive Tavern hub that contrasts the chaotic, god-like battles.

## 3. Core Loop

- Rest: Hang out in the "Boar's Head" Tavern (Hub). Upgrade weapons, check bounties, and interact with physics-based objects (pouring ale, eating food for buffs).
- Deploy: Select a Bounty/Mission from the bounty board.
- Brawl: Enter an arena or story location. Fight waves of magical knights or massive demonic bosses.
- Unleash: Build the "Ultimate Gauge" to trigger anime-style finisher moves.
- Reward: Return to the tavern with gold, wanted posters (XP), and crafting materials.

## 4. Gameplay Systems

### 4.1 VR Combat Mechanics

- The "Full Counter" (Core Mechanic): A precision parry system. Swiping your weapon at the exact moment a magical projectile hits you reflects it back with doubled size/damage.
- Melee: Physics-driven strikes. Enemy armor chips away.
- Dodge/Dash: Quick sidestep (flick stick) leaving an anime-style "afterimage" behind.
- Destructible Environments: Smashing enemies into the ground or walls craters the terrain (crucial for the anime feel).

### 4.2 Weapons & Abilities (The 7 Archetypes)

Inspired directly by the Sins, adapted for unique VR gameplay.

- The Wrath Blade (Meliodas)
	- Style: Broken sword that upgrades to a broadsword. Fast, dual-wielding possible.
	- VR Mechanic: Perfect parry windows. Ultimate surrounds the player in dark purple flames (Hellblaze), turning normal slashes into ranged dark waves.
- The Sloth Spear (King)
	- Style: A floating spear controlled by gestures, not held in the hand.
	- VR Mechanic: Pointing directs the spear to attack. Gestures transform it (Swarm of daggers, Giant sunflower laser, Shield pillow).
- The Envy Hammer (Diane)
	- Style: Massive, two-handed war hammer.
	- VR Mechanic: Extremely heavy to swing (requires two hands). Hitting the ground raises rock pillars to block enemies or launch them.
- The Greed Whip/Fists (Ban)
	- Style: Fast brawling and a bladed whip.
	- VR Mechanic: "Snatch" ability-use the whip to grab enemy shields/weapons out of their hands, or rip "stamina" from enemies to heal yourself.
- The Pride Axe (Escanor)
	- Style: One-handed divine battleaxe.
	- VR Mechanic: "Heat" system. The longer you fight in the light, the stronger you get. Ultimate allows you to throw a massive miniature sun (Cruel Sun) like a baseball.
- The Gluttony Catalyst (Merlin)
	- Style: Spellcasting via a floating orb.
	- VR Mechanic: Draw runes in the air to cast elemental spells.
- The Lust Bow (Gowther)
	- Style: Twin energy bows.
	- VR Mechanic: Archery that fires tracking light-arrows. Hitting specific enemy points "hacks" their minds, making them attack their allies.

### 4.3 Enemy Types (Anime Progression)

- Apprentice Knights: Basic melee fodder.
- Holy Knights (Paladins): Armored enemies that cast their own magic (wind blades, fireballs to be countered).
- Mutated Knights: Knights infused with demonic blood (faster, erratic).
- Red/Gray Demons (Mini-Bosses): Massive enemies that require verticality-hitting their knees to bring them down to eye level.
- Ten Elites (Bosses): Multi-phase encounters with arena-altering ultimate moves.

## 5. Locations

- The Wandering Tavern: (Hub) A cozy pub on the back of a giant pig/creature.
- Fairy Woods: Glowing flora, massive ancient trees.
- Capital Outskirts: Grassy plains with a massive castle in the background.
- Ruined Kingdom: A scorched wasteland filled with demonic energy.

## 6. UI/UX

- Wanted Posters: Instead of a standard menu, level selection is done by grabbing "Wanted Posters" off a wall.
- Bounty/Power Level (Scouter): Look at your wrist to see your current "Power Level" (Score/XP) ticking up dynamically.
- Comic-Book/Manga Impact Frames: On a critical hit, flash a black-and-white high-contrast frame for 0.1 seconds to emphasize impact.

## 7. Art & Audio Direction

- Art Style: Cel-shaded 3D (similar to Genshin Impact or Zelda: Tears of the Kingdom). Bold outlines on characters.
- Key VFX: Anime speed lines in peripheral vision during fast movement.
- Distinct color palettes for magic (Wrath = Purple/Black, Pride = Orange/Yellow, Sloth = Green/Gold).
- Audio: Exaggerated bass drops on heavy impacts.
- Voice acting: Grunts and anime-style attack callouts (optional, adds flavor).
